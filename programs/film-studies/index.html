Film Studies

July 9 - July 27
Residential

Do you have a story you want to tell and put on the big screen? This three-week residential program offers juniors and seniors in high school an immersive introduction to Film Studies. Academic content, field trips, and program activities include:

  • Workshops with industry professionals
  • Hands-on application of key terms, techniques, and genres using digital video equipment
  • A screenwriting workshop with a Hollywood professional
  • Day trip to an on-location shooting
  • Visits to local digital media studios and production houses

Students will attend workshops in film analysis, participate in screening sessions, and work in production labs. They will learn the basics of content development, shooting, and editing; work in small groups to produce short films in both fiction and non-fiction formats; and write film reviews. Previous technical experience is not required.

Meets: Monday - Friday 9:00 am - 5:00 pm / Saturday 10:00 am - 5:00 pm

Faculty Bios

Amy Rust (lead faculty)

An assistant professor in the Humanities & Cultural Studies Department at USF, Dr. Rust earned her Ph. D. in Film Studies at the University of California, Berkeley, in 2010. Her research explores the ways in which visual technologies mediate American cultural experience. She is currently completing a book about the emergence of graphic, corporeal violence in American cinema during the late 1960s and early 1970s. An expert in film genre and visual theory, Dr. Rust regularly teaches courses in international and American film history, documentary and experimental cinema, and the American horror film.

Scott Ferguson (production lab faculty)

An assistant professor in the Humanities & Cultural Studies Department at USF, Dr. Ferguson earned his Ph. D. in Film Studies at the University of California, Berkeley, in 2009. Working at the intersection of visual studies and science studies, his research turns American and European film and new media histories toward explorations of child development and ecology. At USF, Dr. Ferguson has taught courses on the essay film, ecological media, and contemporary American filmmakers in addition to introductory courses in film and new media. Before entering academia, Dr. Ferguson was a professional Hollywood actor for 14 years. A member of the Screen Actors Guild, he has appeared in feature films, television programs, and commercials. Dr. Ferguson has also worked as a professional event videographer and continues to create documentary and experimental works for public exhibition.

Paul Rust (professional screenwriter & actor)

Paul Rust is a Los Angeles-based writer, actor, and comedian. Selected as one of Variety's "10 Comics to Watch" in 2008, Rust has appeared in numerous television programs as well as feature films such as I Love You, Beth Cooper, Inglourious Basterds, and Paper Heart. He also regularly writes, performs, and directs live sketch, improv, and stand-up comedy at the Upright Citizen Brigade Theatre in Los Angeles. As screenwriter, Rust has penned scripts for television (Moral Orel, The Very Funny Show, Late Night with Jimmy Fallon) as well as for feature films, including an as-yet-untitled Pee Wee Herman project. Rust can also be seen performing in two musical groups -- The Glass Beef (a rock-n-roll duo with Charlene Yi) and Don't Stop or We'll Die (a piano-pop trio with Michael Cassady and Harris Wittels) -- and as a featured player on Will Ferrell and Adam McKay's comedy website, Funny or Die.

Daily activites

Dr. Rust will lead the morning workshops in film analysis, during which students will learn basic film history, the differences between narrative, experimental, and documentary cinema, as well as stylistic techniques and how these make meaning for spectators. She will also supervise, along with a teaching assistant, afternoon film screenings and discussion groups that exemplify these concepts and give students an opportunity to discuss them. Students will use what they learn in these film analysis workshops to produce their own films in the afternoon experiential learning sessions described below. Finally, she will lead workshops with students to discuss the various writing assignments they will complete, including a film review, a film sequence analysis, and an academic film essay.

Dr. Ferguson will lead afternoon sessions in experiential learning, during which students will get hands-on experience with the styles and techniques we study and discuss in the mornings and during screening sessions and discussion groups. He will introduce students to scenario writing, shooting, and editing, using digital cameras and editing software procured for these purposes. In the course of these experiential sessions, the students will produce one experimental or documentary short and one narrative short in groups of five. On off-campus field trip will provide students with location shooting experience. Other field trips include trips to local digital production houses.

Finally, Paul Rust, a professional screenwriter and actor in Los Angeles, will join on the afternoon of July 20 for a screenwriting workshop, during which he will introduce students to the art of fiction film writing and supervise their generation of scripts for the narrative short they will begin shooting the next day.
