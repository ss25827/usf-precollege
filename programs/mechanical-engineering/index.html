Mechanical Engineering

June 18 - June 20
Commuter

Interested in a mechanical engineering career with a green twist? This three-day program provides an opportunity for juniors and seniors in high school to work with USF Mechanical Engineering faculty to explore:

  • Sustainable product design
  • Renewable and recyclable materials
  • Cradle-to-cradle product development
  • Carbon footprint software

Sometimes you just have to take something apart and do a life cycle experiment to determine if a product sold as "green" truly is. Faculty from USF's Mechanical Engineering will lead you through an investigation based on the 4 Rs - reduce, reuse, remanufacture, recycle to determine which alternative is more sustainable - example: a power drill or a cordless drill?

Meets: Monday - Wednesday 8:30 am - 5:30 pm
Program Price: $775.00 $600.00

Faculty Bio

Delcie Durham, Ph.D.

Professor Durham's research focuses on environmentally benign design and manufacturing, with a particular emphasis on sustainable product realization through the total product lifecycle. Her research interests include exploring metrics for sustainable engineering design through analogies to the biological cell.
