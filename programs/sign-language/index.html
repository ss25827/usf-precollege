American Sign Language

June 24 - June 29
Residential

This five-day residential program will bring deaf students and hearing American Sign Language (ASL) students together to:

  • Use American Sign Language to discuss issues related to Deaf Culture, American Sign Language, Deaf History, Audiology, and Interpretation
  • Participate in a variety of on-campus and off-campus activities designed to expand learning, leadership, and language skills

Using two classrooms each morning, one for deaf students and one for hearing ASL students, faculty will lead a variety of activities related to deafness, ASL, interpretation and other issues. After lunch, students will unite into one group to view films and participate in indoor and outdoor activities. Evening activities include guest speaker presentations, panels, and one off-campus field trip. This program is targeted to juniors and seniors in high school who are interested in exploring ASL interpretation, counseling the deaf, vocational rehabilitation, deaf education, deaf community organizational work, and deaf legal advocacy.

Meets: Monday - Friday 8:00 am - 9:00 pm
Program Price: $1143.00 $848.00
