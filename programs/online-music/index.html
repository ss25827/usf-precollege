Online Music

June 25 - August 3
Online

Interested in music and earning college credit this summer? The University of South Florida offers online courses designed for high school students entering their sophomore, junior or senior years. The USF Summer Online Music Program for high school students provides students the opportunity to explore interests in music and study with college faculty. No previous formal music training is required and students will be able to build on their musical understanding through online learning and earn transferable college credit.

Courses

The Craft and Business of Songwriting
This is an introductory course on how to write, perform, and market your own songs. The basics of songcrafting will be explored, including the development of a personal style, chord progressions, writing for a personal state or feeling, song forms, exploration of songs that tell a story, the sounds of words and word play, and the basics of recording. Course topics will also include: developing a "songwriter's consciousness" for picking out original ideas; collaborating effectively with co-writers; songwriting for the children's music genre; using the internet for pitching songs, networking, and publicity; and understanding the business of demos and song contracts.

Popular Music in the United States
This course is designed to introduce you to the popular styles that have been a part of the musical culture in the United States. You will make use of both audio and video recordings while examining changes in musical styles with attention to the various stylistic elements associated with various genres including blues, jazz, gospel, Cajun, country, rock, soul, Motown, funk, hip-hop, rap, and digital/electronic. Students will also experiment with sound editing software while investigating the different styles of popular music.

Details

The online six-week program will run from June 25 - August 3 and the cost per course is $648.18. The program is open to qualified high school students in good academic standing. Previous music experience is not required. Applications must be submitted by June 1, 2012.

Faculty Bio

David Williams, Ph.D.

David Williams, Associate Professor of Music Education and Associate Director of the School of music, is coordinator of the Graduate Programs in music. He teaches instrumental music education and technology courses, including: Computer Applications in Music Education. His research projects include Musical Meaning: Teenage Participation in Musical Activities In and Out of School (funded by a "Sounds of Learning: The Impact of Music Education" grant), Collaboration Among Music Teachers, Skills and Behaviors Important to Successful Music Teaching (an insider's look at what it takes), and What are We Doing and How Well are We Doing It? (a philosophical paper published in Music Educators Journal publication).

He published articles in: the Journal of Research in Music Education, Update: Applications of Research to Music Education, Journal of Technology in Music Learning, Southern Music Education Research Journal, Teaching Music, Journal of Music Teacher Education, FETConnection, Technological Directions in Music Learning, and General Music Today, and contributed a book chapter to On the Nature of the Musical Experience.

He recently presented a paper, Enhancing Pre-Service Music Teacher's Learning in Digital Arts Education Through Innovative Approaches to K-12 Classroom Instruction, at a joint national conference of the College Music ociety and the Association for Technology in Music Instruction in Quebec City, Canada.

Williams holds a PhD in music education from Northwestern University and a master of music education degree from the State University of West Georgia. His bachelor degree is in music education from Morehead State University.
