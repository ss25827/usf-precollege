Computer Science

June 21
Commuter

Do you know that computer scientists work in animation, keep planes flying, and assist brain surgeons? In this hands-on, one-day program, high school juniors and seniors learn from USF computer science faculty what computer scientists do by:

  • Building an avatar
  • Creating simple visual applications using Scratch programming language
  • Learning about chip design
  • Looking inside computer hardware, including a microscopic view of computer circuits

At the end of the program, participants will have an avatar, a completed visual Scratch application, and a sound understanding of the career opportunities available to computer scientists.

Meets: Thursday 8:30 am - 5:30 pm
Program Price: $330.00 $273.00

Faculty Bio

Kenneth Christensen, Ph.D., Professor and Undergraduate Program Director

Ken Christensen is a Professor of Computer Science and Engineering at the University of South Florida. He received the Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from North Carolina State University in 1991, M.S. from North Carolina State University in 1983, and B.S. from the university of Florida in 1981. His research and teaching interest is in performance evaluation of computer networks. From 1983 to 1995 he was at IBM Research Triangle Park and held positions in verification and validation of Token Ring LAN and advanced development of future LAN/WAN technologies. He has over 75 conference and journal publications and 13 U.S. patents, all in the areas of computer networks and performance modeling. In 1998 and 1999 he was a NASA summer faculty fellowship at Kennedy Space Center. In 1999 he was awarded a CAREER grant from the National Science Foundation. In 2000 his M.S. student Arun Solleti earned a USF Oustanding Master's Thesis award and in 2001 his Ph.D. student Hiroshi Fujinoki earned a USF Outstanding Dissertation Prize

To date, Ken has advised and graduated 20 M.S. students and five Ph.D. students. The finished M.S. students have gone to careers in industry (including Cisco and IBM) and four of the Ph.D. students have gone on to tenure-track positions at Southern Illinois University Edwardsville, University of North Carolina, University of Arkansas at Little Rock, and Youngstown State University. In 2004 Ken was a visitor in the Department of Communication Systems at Lund University in Sweden. Ken is currently the Editor-in-chief of the International Journal of Network Management (Wiley). He is also on the editorial board for the IEEE IT Professional magazine and Computer Communications journal (Elsevier). He has been a guest co-editor for two special issues of Computer Communications. Ken is on the organizing committee for the IEEE Conference on Local Computer Networks (IEEE LCN) and has hosted the conference in Tampa in 2000, 2001, 2002, and 2004. In 2007, Ken was the General Chair of the Conference (held in Dublin, Ireland). Ken is a licensed Professional Engineer in the State of Florida, a member of ACM and ASEE, and a Senior Member of IEEE.
