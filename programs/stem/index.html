    Overview

    The Summer 2012 STEM Pre-College Program challenges high school students entering Grades 9 through 12, as well as graduating seniors, to explore the fields of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics well beyond the high school level. Gifted students will be given the opportunity to explore these fields from multiple perspectives:

    • Participating students will gain the perspective of a college student through their experiences in university courses.
      • These courses help in preparing students for university studies and easing the future transition to college life. Through the program, many students are able to earn high school and college credits early in their academic careers, setting them ahead in their field of study.
    • All instructors are experienced research professors from the University of South Florida.
      • Studying under accomplished university professors gives students an early experience with the format of college level courses. This helps participants to discover, at an early stage, the individual strategies for learning in a University environment.
    • Students will have the opportunity to experience the STEM fields from the perspective of researchers, dealing with real-world problems.
      • Research work is a required component of college students' resumes in the STEM fields, especially for those students intending to continue to graduate school. Participating students will get a jump start on the challenges of the STEM fields and experience the voyage of discovery that is continually evolving in these fields.
    • Program participants will also experience the STEM fields through a hands-on approach.
      • These experiences allow students to connect with both the STEM fields and with one another. Students leaving the program in previous years have created lasting friendships. Many graduates of the program have discovered interests and pursued careers in STEM fields as well as created lasting friendships.

    The STEM Education Center will host intelligent, motivated, and gifted students on the Tampa campus of the University of South Florida. The six-week program is designed to excite, challenge, and foster creative thinking, discovery and learning. The Program consists of two levels:

    • Level I is designed for students entering grades 9 and 10.
    • Level II is for students entering grades 11, 12, and graduating seniors.

    The programs for level I and level II begins on June 11, 2012 and continues through July 20, 2012. Classes meet Monday through Friday, from 9:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m.

    For more information on the Level I and II programs, please see the tabs above.

    The deadline to submit an application is May 7, 2012.

  • Level I:

    This level is for students entering grades 9 and 10. To be eligible students must have had a course in Algebra 1. Three disciplines will be studied:

    • Mathematics: Topics from finite mathematics, combinatorics, probability, and elementary calculus augmented with research projects;
    • Computer Science: Computer language with applications in physical sciences and engineering;
    • Biology/Physics: Selected topics from biology, environmental science, and physics augmented with research projects.

    College Credit

    Additional courses will be offered for students seeking to earn college credit from the University. These courses may be cost free to students through dual enrollment supported by USF Honors College and the STEM program.

    Level I students will generally be eligible to take PreCalculus (MAC 1147) for 4 college credits. However, students who have taken calculus or present the following prerequisites may be eligible to take Engineering Calculus (MAC 2281) for 4 college credits.

    SAT Quantitative Minimum Score: 550
    or EACT Math Minimum Score: 24
    or CPT College Level Math Minimum Score: 060
    or MAC 1105 Minimum Grade: C

    This will be determined after the students application has been accepted, there will be no difference in the application process for level I students intending to take either course.

    Recreational Activities

    Students will have the opportunity to visit and participate weekly in a variety of hands on activities including engineering and biology labs, visits to the USF Graphic Arts studio, University Honors College, MOSI, and an annual chess tournament. Possible visits to the Morsani School of Medicine and Kennedy Space Center are being explored. In addition, the facilities at USF such as the University Library, Marshall Student Center, and the Campus Recreation Center will be made available to program participants.

    Cost of Program

    The cost of the Level I program is $750 plus a $50 application fee. This fee is due at the time of application.

    Financial Aid

    Financial aid is available to students with demonstrated need. To apply for financial aid you must send your parent's tax return (their Social Security numbers can be whited out) and a statement explaining your financial situation, including why aid is important for you to be able to participate in the program, to the STEM Education Center. If accepted into the program, we will review your financial information, and if we are able to award you aid, will notify you of the amount.

    The deadline to submit an application is May 7, 2012

  • Level II

    This level is for students entering grades 11 and 12 as well as those completing the 12th grade.

    Three disciplines will be studied:

    • Mathematics: Linear Algebra and/or Engineering Calculus and/or Bridge to Abstract Math(See College credit options below);
    • Computer Science: Computer language with engineering applications in computer graphics, animation, and pattern recognition;
    • Physics/Biology: Selected topics in the areas of nanobiotechnology, nanomedicine, and environmental science augmented with research projects.

    College Credit

    Additional courses will be offered for students seeking to earn college credit from the University. These courses may be cost free to students through dual enrollment supported by USF Honors College and the STEM program.

    • Engineering Calculus, MAC 2281 (4) credits
    • Linear Algebra, MAS 3105 (4) credits
    • Bridge to Abstract Mathematics, MGF 3301 (4) credits

    Level II students may take a unique combination of the above courses based on their individual interests and mathematics background. This will be determined after acceptance into the program, there will be no difference in the application process for level II students intending to take one or multiple of the above courses.

    Recreational Activities

    Students will have the opportunity to visit and participate weekly in a variety of hands on activities including engineering and biology labs, visits to the USF Graphic Arts studio, University Honors College, MOSI, and an annual chess tournament. Possible visits to the Morsani School of Medicine and Kennedy Space Center are being explored. In addition, the facilities at USF such as the University Library, Marshall Student Center, and the Campus Recreation Center will be made available to program participants.

    Cost of Program

    The cost of the Level II program is $750 plus a $50 application fee. Scholarships from the Academy of Applied Science may also be available for outstanding Level II students to engage in research on a one-on-one basis with a USF faculty mentor.

    Financial Aid

    Financial aid is available to students with demonstrated need. To apply for financial aid you must send your parent's tax return (their Social Security numbers can be whited out) and a statement explaining your financial situation, including why aid is important for you to be able to participate in the program, to the STEM Education Center. If accepted into the program, we will review your financial information, and if we are able to award you aid, will notify you of the amount.

    The deadline to submit an application is May 7, 2012
